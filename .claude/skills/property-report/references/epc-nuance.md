# EPC nuance — what the certificate actually tells you

Load this reference when the user asks about EPC, energy efficiency, MEES compliance, retrofit cost, or when EPC data shows up in a way that needs interpreting.

## The grades, plainly

| Grade | Score (SAP) | Rough read |
|---|---|---|
| A | 92+ | Rare outside new-build flats to Passivhaus standard |
| B | 81–91 | Very efficient. New-build or deep retrofit |
| C | 69–80 | The MEES-compliant floor for rentals from 2028 (see below) |
| D | 55–68 | Typical UK housing stock. Compliant today for rental, not 2028 |
| E | 39–54 | Current MEES floor for rentals (2020 regulations) |
| F | 21–38 | Below current rental floor — letting usually prohibited |
| G | 0–20 | Same — cannot let without an exemption |

Rule of thumb: most UK housing stock sits in D or E. A BTL at D today is compliant but has retrofit exposure when the rental floor rises.

## MEES — the regulation to watch

**Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards.** Current rule: residential lettings need EPC E or higher. Registered exemptions aside, an F or G rental is not lettable.

**Proposed rise to C by 2028** (for new tenancies) and **by 2030 for all tenancies**. This was signalled by government then delayed, then reignited. As of the current market cycle, treat EPC D on a rental as a flagged renovation cost that may need spending within 2-4 years.

Cap on improvement cost: £15,000 per property in the current proposals (up from £3,500 in older drafts). Check the most recent DESNZ guidance before quoting a client.

## Current rating vs potential rating

The EPC shows two grades:
- **Current rating** — what the property is now
- **Potential rating** — what it would be after the measures listed in the certificate (usually loft insulation, cavity wall, double glazing upgrade, heat pump, PV)

If current is D and potential is C, the property has a realistic path to compliance. If current is F and potential is C, you're looking at meaningful works.

The certificate lists each recommended measure with **indicative cost** and **annual saving**. Treat the cost figure as a floor — real quotes typically run 20-40% higher, especially for heat pumps post-2023.

## When to flag a floor-area mismatch

The EPC carries a floor area in square metres. If the Rightmove listing quotes a different size, something is off:

- **EPC smaller than listing (e.g. EPC 80 sqm, listing 168 sqm):** common when an extension was added after the EPC was issued. The EPC is stale. Tell the user — the energy cost figures won't reflect the full property.
- **EPC larger than listing:** less common. May be a wrong-address match (shared postcode), conversion (HMO split back to single dwelling), or bad listing.
- **Match within 10%:** treat as consistent.

Always mention the mismatch in the report rather than silently using the EPC figure.

## Retrofit cost, roughly

Useful anchors when someone asks "what would it cost to get this to C":

| Measure | Indicative cost | Typical lift |
|---|---|---|
| Loft insulation (top-up to 270mm) | £400 – £1,000 | +2-5 SAP points |
| Cavity wall insulation | £1,000 – £3,000 | +5-10 SAP points |
| Solid wall insulation (internal) | £8,000 – £15,000 | +10-20 SAP points |
| Double glazing (whole house) | £4,000 – £10,000 | +2-5 SAP points |
| Air-source heat pump + cylinder | £8,000 – £14,000 (pre-grant) | +5-15 SAP points |
| Solar PV (4kW system) | £5,000 – £8,000 | +5-10 SAP points |

These are ballpark. Always suggest the user gets real quotes before budgeting. Heat pumps in particular vary wildly based on radiator rebalancing needs.

## EPC data quality caveats

- Certificates expire after 10 years. A 2014 cert on a 2024 sale is still "valid" but practically stale.
- Self-assessment doesn't exist — only accredited domestic energy assessors (DEA) can issue.
- The Open Data EPC register (epc.opendatacommunities.org) is authoritative. Rightmove EPC badges sometimes lag.
- Listed buildings and churches can be exempt — no EPC needed. Not a signal of inefficiency.

## What NOT to say

- Don't quote an exact retrofit cost — say "indicative" or give a range
- Don't promise MEES 2028 will happen on schedule — it's been delayed before
- Don't treat an EPC C as "efficient" in absolute terms — it's the regulatory floor for rentals, not a high bar
